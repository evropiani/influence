Influence

A quick-round land grab. Paint the most ground before the clock runs out — or wipe everyone off the map.

The idea in 10 seconds

You spend influence to claim cells and turn the map your colour. Land and captured nodes fund you. Whoever holds the most territory when time runs out wins.

Controls

On a computer
  • Click toward where you want to grow — your nearest border pushes that way.
  • Drag to pan, mouse wheel to zoom. WASD or arrow keys pan too.
  • Minimap (corner): click to jump across the map.
On a phone
  • Tap toward where you want to grow.
  • One finger drag to pan, pinch to zoom.
  • Tap the minimap to jump.

How it works

  • Influence is your currency (top-left). It refills on its own, and every point spends as one cell of empty land.
  • The Commit slider sets how much of your influence each tap spends. Slide low for careful pokes, high to throw everything at one push.
  • Nodes are the ringed points dotted around the map. Grow your territory over one to capture it. Nodes are your economy — they pay influence over time, raise your influence limit, and act as supply points. Grabbing one also drops an instant bonus (bigger for stealing an enemy's).
  • Your influence has a cap (shown as X / limit) that grows as you capture nodes. You can't hoard a giant reserve, so getting big first doesn't let you sit back and steamroll — you have to spend well.

Fighting & entrenchment

Empty land costs 1 influence per cell. Taking an enemy's cell costs more — and the longer they've held it, the tougher it gets. You can see this at a glance: fresh land is bright, dug-in land is darker.

Darkdug in · tough · expensive

So aim for bright, soft seams and avoid grinding straight into a dark, fortified core. Capturing an enemy's node is the real prize — a fat influence bonus, and it starves their economy.

Supply lines

Pushing far from your own nodes costs extra per cell — you can't lunge clear across the map in one move. To reach further, capture nodes along the way: every node you own widens the area you can expand into cheaply.

Build outward, node by node — don't overreach.

Your base

The ringed marker sitting in your territory is your base. Hang on to it — if an enemy captures it, your income drops by 30% until you build a new one.

To rebuild: hold a node long enough that it's fully dug in (its cell reaches maximum entrenchment), then tap it and pay 300 influence. It turns into your new base and your income is restored. Nodes you can convert will glow, so you always know your options.

Quick tips

  • Grab the nodes near you first. They pay for everything and extend how far you can reach.
  • Read the colours: bright edges are weak points, dark ones are fortified.
  • A capped bar spent far from your nodes buys almost nothing. Set up a forward node first, then strike.
  • Borders you've held a while are cheap to defend and costly to crack — patience pays.
